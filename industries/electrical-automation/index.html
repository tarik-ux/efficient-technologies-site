// illustrative workflow

From an electrical inquiry to the correct owner

This is an example system design, not a customer result. The audit documents service area, job categories, residential and commercial routing, safety language, estimate ownership, and scheduling rules.

  1. Capture the inquiry

    A missed call or web submission creates one record with source, timestamp, and available contact details.

  2. Acknowledge receipt

    An approved response confirms the request without offering technical advice or promising a time slot.

  3. Collect broad job context

    Agreed questions can distinguish service, installation, estimate, residential, or commercial requests.

  4. Apply routing rules

    Service area, job category, safety language, and account type determine the approved queue or human escalation.

  5. Create the estimate or scheduling handoff

    The correct office owner receives the context and next action in the existing system of record.

  6. Stop and expose exceptions

    Reply, booking, opt-out, invalid data, or attempt limit stops the sequence; failed or unresolved items remain visible.

Technical judgment remains human: the system can organize intake and routing, but electrical advice, code interpretation, pricing, estimating, and safety decisions are outside the automated step.

// questions

Electrical automation FAQ

Can automation answer technical electrical questions?

No. It can collect broad intake information and route it; licensed judgment, safety guidance, code questions, and diagnosis remain with people.

Can residential and commercial inquiries follow different paths?

Yes. The workflow can apply supplied categories and ownership rules, with unclear records routed to a human.

Can it follow up on estimates?

Yes, through a bounded, approved sequence that respects consent and stops on reply, opt-out, or the agreed attempt limit.

Will it work with our current CRM or field-service tool?

The audit checks the actual integration surface, permissions, data fields, and fallback behavior before making that commitment.

What the Lead Recovery System does

The exact timing and channels depend on the business, consent rules, and tools already in use. The operating pattern stays explicit.

  1. Detect the enquiry

    An unanswered call, contact form, chat, or approved lead source creates one event.

  2. Acknowledge it

    The system sends the agreed acknowledgement without pretending that a technician has already accepted the job.

  3. Capture useful context

    Service type, location, urgency, preferred time, and contact details are collected only when relevant.

  4. Route and record

    The opportunity reaches the agreed inbox, CRM stage, dispatcher, or owner with a visible status.

  5. Follow the approved sequence

    Unanswered opportunities receive a bounded sequence with stop conditions, not endless messages.

  6. Hand control to a person

    A reply, exception, urgent request, or booked appointment stops or changes the automation according to the approved rules.

Illustrative workflow, not a customer result: a missed service call can trigger an acknowledgement, collect the caller's ZIP code and job type, alert the office, and stop automatically when the office replies.

// fit and boundaries

What we connect—and what must be decided first

Typical inputs

Business phone events, website forms, approved lead sources, inboxes, and lightweight CRM records.

Typical outputs

Acknowledgements, internal alerts, lead stages, tasks, booking links, and a documented human handoff.

Customer decisions

Service area, business hours, emergency language, routing owner, contact cadence, and stop conditions.

System limits

The workflow cannot guarantee job fit, availability, customer response, or a booked job. Those remain business outcomes.

// questions

Lead recovery FAQ

Do we need to replace our CRM or phone system?

No. The audit starts with the tools already in use. Replacement is proposed only when an existing tool cannot support the agreed workflow.

Does the automation answer technical or emergency questions?

Not by default. It can acknowledge and collect context, but urgent or technical decisions route to the responsible person under the approved rules.

How do we know whether it works?

We define observable states such as acknowledged, routed, replied, booked, closed, or stopped. Customer revenue is measured separately from workflow operation.

Can we start with only missed calls?

Yes. Launch is designed for one tightly scoped workflow, which can be expanded only after the first system is stable.

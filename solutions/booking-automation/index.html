// workflow

How a booking workflow is controlled

The system follows the business's actual capacity and service rules. It does not invent availability or dispatch authority.

  1. Receive the request

    A booking link, recovered lead, phone follow-up, or form starts the workflow.

  2. Apply fit rules

    Service area, job type, duration, lead time, and required information determine the next step.

  3. Offer valid availability

    The customer sees only approved times—or submits a request for office confirmation when direct booking is not appropriate.

  4. Confirm and record

    The appointment or request is written to the agreed calendar, CRM, and internal notification path.

  5. Remind and reschedule

    Bounded reminders and a clear rescheduling path reduce manual coordination.

  6. Handle exceptions

    No-shows, urgent requests, capacity conflicts, and unusual jobs route back to a person.

Illustrative workflow, not a customer result: an after-hours maintenance request can collect the ZIP code and service type, show approved appointment windows, write the selection to the calendar, and alert the office for review.

// design choices

Direct booking is not always the right answer

Use direct booking when

The service, duration, coverage area, capacity, and required intake data are predictable.

Use request-first when

A dispatcher or technician must confirm urgency, scope, travel, parts, or staffing.

Preserve the source of truth

One approved calendar or scheduling system owns availability; the automation does not maintain a competing calendar.

Define stop conditions

Cancellation, rescheduling, staff takeover, and customer replies stop or alter the sequence.

// questions

Booking automation FAQ

Can this work with our existing calendar?

The audit checks its availability rules, API or integration support, and ownership model. If it can remain the source of truth, we connect to it rather than replace it.

Can emergency jobs book automatically?

Only if the business defines an appropriate and truthful rule. Most emergency or ambiguous requests should acknowledge the customer and alert a person.

Will reminders stop when someone cancels?

Yes, when cancellation status is available to the workflow. Stop and exception states are acceptance criteria, not optional polish.

Does booking automation guarantee fewer no-shows?

No. It guarantees the agreed reminder and recovery workflow operates; customer behavior remains an outcome to measure.
